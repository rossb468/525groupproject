8 Bit

Starting with 8-bit games, this style features simple characters and backgrounds using a limited number of colors. This graphics style started in the 1970s and went into the 1980s. The pixels are very visible in 8-bit graphics. Notice the simple backgrounds and characters in the following video game examples.

8-bit gaming systems include the Atari 2600, the Nintendo NES, and the Nintendo Game Boy.

Popular games include Pong, the first arcade game, which was later developed into a television version for at-home play. On the Atari 2600, Frogger was a popular 8-bit game.

Many arcade games, including Donkey Kong, had 8-bit graphics.

And the early Pokémon games also had 8-bit graphics, including Pokémon Yellow.
